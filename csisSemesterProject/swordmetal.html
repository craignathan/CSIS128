Sword Metal's

Stainless Steel

Stainless Steel Stainless steel is a basic steel used for production of almost every sword sold on the market. It is a realatively cheap steel to use to make for decorative swords. The reason stainless steel is used for decorative swords is becuase any blad over 12" long is considered to be to brittle for any real usage. However, if used as a practice sword with not contact with anything hard it will work. Stainless Steel gets it's name from the high Chromium content which gives its Stainless ability. The Chromium content of stainless steel is usually over 11%.

Carbon Steel

(High) Carbon Steel Carbon Steel is bare minimun steel needed to be used for real sword production. Carbon steel is classified by a 10 usually followed by a number between 1-99. The last 2 digits of the number tell what percentage of carbon the steel contains. For example 1045 carbon steel has a .45% carbon level so the last 2 digits tell you that it is between .01 and .99% carbon. For sword's there are 3 main types of steel used 1045, 1060, 1095 carbon steel. While using carbon steel the most ideal range of carbon content is between .5 and .7 carbon content.
1045 Carbon Steel 1045 is a cheap carbon steel and is used quite often to make cheaper swords. 1045 carbon steel is the lowest possible carbon content .45% accepatble for a funcational sword.
1060 Carbon Steel 1060 Carbon Steel is great steel to be used when producing swords. It's hardness(blade retention) and pliability(strength) is suitable for most swords. But does cost more than a 1045 Carbon Steel sword due to its more higher difficulty to forge.
1095 Carbon Steel 1095 Carbon is a very hard steel if heat treated properly. 1095 Carbon Steel is the hardess(blade retention) carbon steel you can get for a sword. However, the swords are much more pricey than 1045 or 1060 Carbon Steel swords and it trades off its durabilty for the hardness of the sword. Meaning 1060 has a stronger durabilty than 1095 steel but doesnt retain sharpness as well as 1095. 1095 steel may be more fragile than lower carbon steels but it is still a hard steel.

Spring Steel

Spring Steels With spring steel there are two types of spring steel's mainly used for swords. 5160 and 9260 steel like carbon steel the last 2 digits display the carbon content of the sword so like 1060 Carbon Steel they both have .60% carbon content. Spring steel when properly heat treated can be very flexible and allow the sword to bend very far and return to its original position.
5160 Spring Steel 5160 Spring Steel is a low Chromium alloy steel. It contain approximately .7% Chromium However, that is not enough to make it stainless steel(requires 13% Chromium). It is also combined with about .2% Silicon which allows the the sword to be very durable and bend a bit and return to its original shape.
9260 Spring Steel 9260 Spring Steel is a very bendable metal and is also called Silicon Manganese Steel. It consists of 2% silicon content which allows it to be even more resilient thatn 5160 Spring Steel. It can bend to nearly 90 degree angle and still retain its original shape.

Tool Steel

Tool Steels Tool steels are a popular steel for swords due to it's ability to retain a sharp edge nad still be very strong. The two main types of tool steel used for swords are T-10 Tool Steel and L6 Bainite.
T-10 Tool Steel T10 Tool Steel is a Tungsten alloy Steel with a very high carbon content(approximate .9 to 1.0%) and .35% maximum silicon content. T-10 Tool Steel is a very tough steel and is also very resistant to scratches and abrasions.
L6 Bainite L6 Bainite Tool Steel that is also used with band saw steels. The L tells you that it is a low alloy steel. When properly heat treated it is known as one of the toughest steels o nthe market. Most swords made with L6 Bainite are usually never under $1,000.
S7 Shock Steel S7 Shock Steel is a shock resistant steel and is also shares many characteristics with L6 Tool Steel when heat treated Properly. S7 Shock Steel is rarely used in sword production so it make it a very sought after Steel.

Damacus Steel/Folded Steel

Damascus Steel/Folded Steel Damascus Steel is another term for folded Steel. Most Damascus swords dont come under a price tage of $500 and is the older way of how Japanese Katana sword smith's use to make there swords. If not folded corectly it can be considered weaker than 440 Stainless Steel.
